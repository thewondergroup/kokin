01 Philosophy
古今

A cuisine rooted in heritage, always evolving.

"Kokin is where time converges — informing every choice, every flavour, every moment."

The character 古 (ko) represents the old — history, depth, and tradition. 今 (kin) means now — freshness, movement, and the beauty of the present moment.

Together they form Kokin: a tribute to the timeless spirit of Japanese cuisine. Traditional techniques honoured. Contemporary sensibility embraced. A fire-born tradition, reborn in the heart of modern London.

02 The Chef

Daisuke
Shimoyama

下山 大輔
Refining kaiseki through fire and philosophy.

Chef Shimoyama's cooking is shaped by the mountains of Gunma — the warmth, scent, and rhythm of burning pine that formed his earliest food memories. Over two decades he has carried those memories through some of the world's most exacting kitchens, including Michelin three-starred Ryugin in Tokyo and Michelin-starred Umu in London.

Kokin is his bold and elemental vision: Japanese cuisine shaped by fire, guided by seasonality, and grounded in deep respect for every ingredient.

Fire is not a
technique —
it is an ingredient.

At the centre of Kokin is woodfire: primal, intimate, and rich with memory. Cherry and apple wood infuse delicate smoke. Binchotan charcoal brings clean, high heat. Every flame has a purpose.

— 01

Woodfire

木火 · Kibi

Cherry and apple wood infuse the dishes with delicate, fragrant smoke — a warmth that evokes childhood kitchens and mountain fires.

— 02

Binchotan

備長炭 · White charcoal

A clean, sustained high heat that draws out clarity and texture from each ingredient — a technique perfected across centuries.

— 03

Bluefin

本鮪 · Hon-maguro

Whole wild tuna from Spain and Portugal, broken down in-house with precision and reverence — otoro to kama, nothing wasted.
